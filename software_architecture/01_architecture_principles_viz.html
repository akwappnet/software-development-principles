Layered Architecture
üè¢ Like a Modern Office Building
Each floor has a specific purpose: Reception (UI), Management (Business Logic), Archives (Data). You don't skip floors - you go through proper channels!
Organize your software into horizontal layers where each layer only talks to the layer directly below it. This creates clear separation of concerns and makes the system easier to maintain.

Clean Architecture Rules
üèõÔ∏è Like a Medieval Castle
The treasure (core business) is in the center, protected by walls. Outer walls can be rebuilt, but the treasure stays safe and unchanged!
Keep your business rules at the center, independent of frameworks, databases, and UI. Dependencies should point inward - outer layers depend on inner layers, never the reverse.

Microservices Principles
üè™ Like a Shopping Mall
Each store (service) is independent - has its own staff, inventory, and cash register. If the bookstore closes, the coffee shop keeps running!
Break your application into small, independent services that communicate over well-defined APIs. Each service can be developed, deployed, and scaled independently.
